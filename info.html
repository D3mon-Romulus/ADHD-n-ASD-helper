üéØ ADHD (Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder)

What is ADHD?

ADHD is a neurodevelopmental condition that affects how the brain develops and functions. It's not a lack of willpower or laziness - it's a real difference in brain structure and chemistry.

Common Signs:

  • Inattention: Difficulty focusing, easily distracted, forgetting things
  • Hyperactivity: Feeling restless, fidgeting, talking a lot
  • Impulsivity: Acting without thinking, difficulty waiting turns

üí° Did You Know?

ADHD affects about 1 in 20 children and often continues into adulthood. Many successful people have ADHD, including entrepreneurs, athletes, and artists!

Strengths of People with ADHD:

  • Creative thinking and problem-solving
  • High energy and enthusiasm
  • Ability to hyperfocus on interesting topics
  • Spontaneity and fun-loving nature
  • Resilience and adaptability

üåà ASD (Autism Spectrum Disorder)

What is ASD?

ASD is a neurodevelopmental condition that affects social communication and behavior. It's called a "spectrum" because it affects people in many different ways and to varying degrees.

Common Characteristics:

  • Social Communication: Difficulty with social interactions and non-verbal communication
  • Repetitive Behaviors: Repeating movements, words, or activities
  • Sensory Differences: Over- or under-sensitivity to sounds, lights, textures
  • Routine Preferences: Liking predictable schedules and routines

üåü Remember:

Every person with autism is unique. Some may need more support with daily activities, while others live independently and have successful careers.

Strengths of People with ASD:

  • Attention to detail and accuracy
  • Strong memory for facts and information
  • Honest and direct communication
  • Deep expertise in areas of interest
  • Logical and systematic thinking
  • Loyalty and reliability

üìÖ Structure & Routine

  • Use visual schedules and calendars
  • Create consistent daily routines
  • Break big tasks into smaller steps
  • Use timers and reminders

üéØ Focus & Attention

  • Minimize distractions in work areas
  • Use fidget tools or stress balls
  • Take regular movement breaks
  • Try the Pomodoro technique (work in short bursts)

üòå Self-Regulation

  • Practice deep breathing exercises
  • Create a calm-down space
  • Use sensory tools (weighted blankets, noise-canceling headphones)
  • Learn to recognize emotional triggers

üí™ Building Strengths

  • Celebrate small victories
  • Focus on interests and talents
  • Use strengths to overcome challenges
  • Build on what works well

üîç Common Myths vs Facts

‚ùå Myth: ADHD and autism are caused by bad parenting or too much screen time.

‚úÖ Fact: These are neurological conditions with genetic and biological causes. They're not anyone's fault!

‚ùå Myth: People with ADHD just need to try harder to focus.

‚úÖ Fact: ADHD brains work differently. People with ADHD may need different strategies and support to succeed.

‚ùå Myth: All people with autism are the same.

‚úÖ Fact: Autism is a spectrum - each person has unique strengths, challenges, and support needs.

‚ùå Myth: Children will "grow out of" ADHD and autism.

‚úÖ Fact: These are lifelong conditions, but people can learn strategies to thrive and succeed!

  • Paediatrician referrals covered under Medicare
